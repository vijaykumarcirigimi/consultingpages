Frequently asked questions on L&D consulting

Everything you need to know about partnering with Edstellar

How can organizations determine their L&D needs?

+

We conduct a comprehensive Training Needs Analysis (TNA) that includes skills gap assessments, stakeholder interviews, performance data review, and competency mapping. This data-driven approach identifies priority areas where L&D investment will have the greatest impact on business outcomes.

How do you measure the effectiveness of L&D programs?

+

We use a multi-level evaluation framework that measures reaction, learning, behaviour change, and business impact. Each engagement includes defined KPIs, baseline measurements, and post-program assessments to quantify ROI and demonstrate the connection between learning activity and performance improvement.

What is the typical timeline for an L&D consulting engagement?

+

Timelines vary based on scope. A focused skills gap analysis can be completed in 4–6 weeks, while a full L&D strategy redesign typically takes 3–6 months. We begin every engagement with a scoping workshop to align on deliverables, milestones, and timeline expectations before work begins.

Can we start with a small project before committing to a larger engagement?

+

Absolutely. Many of our clients begin with a diagnostic engagement — a focused assessment or pilot program that delivers immediate value while giving both sides a clear picture of the opportunity. This low-risk starting point often evolves into a broader strategic partnership.

What makes Edstellar different from other L&D consulting firms?

+

Three things set us apart: our global network of 5,000+ trainers across 100+ locations gives us unmatched delivery scale; our consulting transfers expertise rather than creating dependency; and our frameworks are built on governed, auditable data — not assumptions or self-reported skill claims.
