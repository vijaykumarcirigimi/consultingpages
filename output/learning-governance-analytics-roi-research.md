### 3. Kirkpatrick Partners — The Kirkpatrick Model
- **URL:** https://www.kirkpatrickpartners.com/the-kirkpatrick-model/
- **Headline:** "The Standard for Leveraging and Validating Talent Investments"
- **Pain points addressed:** Organizations measuring activity not outcomes; difficulty connecting learning to business results; oversimplification of causal relationships in training evaluation; lack of environmental support undermining learning transfer
- **Services/capabilities listed:**
  - Kirkpatrick Four-Level Evaluation Model with modern extensions
  - Certification programs (globally recognized)
  - Kaddie — AI-powered instructional design tool built on Kirkpatrick principles
  - Consulting and impact studies
  - Network of 14,000+ certified professionals for hire
- **Stats used:** 14,000+ certified professionals globally; 70+ years of proven effectiveness; used across government, military, corporate, consulting, humanitarian sectors
- **Differentiators claimed:** World's most widely used framework for measuring learning impact; modern extensions beyond traditional ROI — Return on Expectations (ROE), Return on Performance (ROP), Contributive ROI (cROI) creating "Integrated Return"; emphasis on Performance Environment as foundation; backward design from Level 4 outcomes; AI-powered Kaddie tool
- **Process/methodology:** Four levels: (1) Reaction — favorable, engaging, relevant, (2) Learning — knowledge, skills, attitude, confidence, commitment, (3) Behavior — critical behaviors in real environments measured within 90 days, (4) Results — targeted organizational outcomes. Model operates cyclically, starting from Level 4 and designing backward.
- **Notable content:** Certification programs, community of practice, Kaddie AI tool, consulting network

## Synthesis

### Common patterns (table stakes)
- **Multi-level evaluation framework** — Every competitor references Kirkpatrick (4 levels) or Phillips (5 levels) as the backbone of their measurement approach. Edstellar must demonstrate fluency in these models.
- **Business impact focus** — All competitors frame measurement as connecting learning to business outcomes, not tracking completions. The shift from "activity metrics" to "impact metrics" is universal messaging.
- **Dashboard/reporting** — Custom analytics dashboards aggregating data across learning ecosystems appear in every competitor offering.
- **Isolation of training impact** — Credible competitors (ROI Institute, GP Strategies) emphasize statistical methods to isolate training's contribution from other business variables.
- **Capability building** — GP Strategies (Measurement Academy), ROI Institute (Certification), and Kirkpatrick (Certification) all offer programs to build internal measurement capability, not just deliver consulting.
- **Proprietary frameworks/tools** — Each competitor has a named methodology: Measurement Map® (GP Strategies), Phillips ROI Methodology (ROI Institute), Integrated Return (Kirkpatrick), Six-Category Framework (Deloitte), Quotient + Success Case Method (STCCG).

### Gaps & opportunities
- **Learning Governance is underserved** — No competitor has a dedicated page or structured offering for L&D governance frameworks. Governance is mentioned tangentially (Deloitte blog, Brandon Hall research) but nobody owns it as a consulting service. This is Edstellar's biggest differentiation opportunity.
- **Integrated governance + analytics + ROI** — Competitors treat measurement/analytics and governance as separate topics. Edstellar can own the narrative that governance is the foundation that makes analytics and ROI possible — you can't measure what you don't govern.
- **AI-powered learning analytics** — Only Kirkpatrick (Kaddie tool) and Deloitte mention AI. Most competitors still position analytics as manual/traditional. Opportunity to lead with AI-powered analytics as a differentiator.
- **Data strategy for L&D** — None of the competitors frame "learning data strategy" as a service — defining what data to collect, where to store it, how to integrate it, and who owns it. This is a gap Edstellar can fill.
- **Maturity assessment** — Brandon Hall research shows only 27% of companies have a learning measurement framework and only 10% are at "innovative" maturity. A maturity assessment tool is an obvious lead magnet that no competitor prominently offers as a gated asset.
- **Predictive analytics** — Deloitte mentions it but doesn't productize it. No competitor offers predictive learning analytics (forecasting skill gaps, predicting program impact before launch) as a named service.
- **Cost of non-measurement** — Competitors focus on the benefits of measuring but rarely quantify the cost of NOT measuring. Industry stats (only 13% measure ROI, 10-20% skill transfer rates) create a powerful "burning platform" angle.

### Best-in-class examples
- **GP Strategies** — Best service page structure: clear service cards, named proprietary tools (Measurement Map®, Measurement Academy™), four-step process, industry recognition proof point. Most actionable and scannable.
- **ROI Institute** — Best proof points: 14,000+ certified professionals, 5,000 annual impact studies, 6,000+ organizations, 70+ countries. Unmatched in scale of adoption stats.
- **Deloitte** — Best use of industry research: 95% stat, 69% skills gap stat, six-category framework. Most credible "thought leadership" positioning.
- **Kirkpatrick Partners** — Best framework modernization: extending beyond basic ROI to ROE, ROP, cROI. Shows the model is evolving, not static. AI tool (Kaddie) is forward-looking.
- **STCCG** — Best practical framing: three simple questions that every L&D leader asks. Accessible entry point before diving into methodology.
