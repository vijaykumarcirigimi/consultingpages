Frequently asked questions on learning technology consulting

Everything you need to know about Edstellar's learning technology consulting services

What is learning technology consulting?

+

Learning technology consulting is the strategic advisory service that helps organizations select, implement, and optimize their learning technology stack — including LMS, LXP, authoring tools, analytics platforms, and integrations — aligned to business goals, learner needs, and IT requirements. It goes beyond platform selection to address data flows, integration patterns, governance frameworks, and long-term scalability.

What does LMS selection and implementation involve?

+

LMS selection involves a vendor-neutral evaluation across 40+ criteria including functional fit, integration capability, scalability, security, and total cost of ownership. Implementation covers end-to-end system configuration — user roles, course structure, SCORM/xAPI content import, HRIS integrations, reporting dashboards, testing, and administrator training. Edstellar manages the full process with no vendor bias.

Can Edstellar integrate learning technology with our existing HR systems?

+

Yes. Edstellar designs integration architectures connecting your learning platform to HRIS, talent management, performance management, and skills intelligence systems. We map data flows, define API requirements, configure SSO, and ensure unified reporting across your HR technology ecosystem — including SAP SuccessFactors, Workday, Oracle HCM, and Microsoft Viva.

What is the difference between an LMS and an LXP?

+

An LMS (Learning Management System) manages structured, compliance-driven training delivery — assignments, completions, certifications, and regulatory tracking. An LXP (Learning Experience Platform) enables self-directed, AI-curated content discovery, social learning, and personalised learning paths. Most enterprises benefit from both, and Edstellar helps architect the optimal configuration for your organization.

How long does a learning technology consulting engagement take?

+

A full engagement — from discovery audit through vendor evaluation and architecture blueprint — typically runs 8–12 weeks. Discovery and audit: 2–3 weeks. Requirements and design: 3–4 weeks. Vendor evaluation: 2–4 weeks. Implementation support is ongoing. Organizations with simpler requirements or an existing platform seeking optimization can complete in 4–6 weeks.

What emerging learning technologies does Edstellar support?

+

Edstellar supports VR and AR immersive learning, gamification and simulation-based learning, AI-powered learning automation and intelligent content curation, mobile learning solutions for distributed workforces, blockchain-based credentialing and certification, and educational technology integration. Our consultants help organizations evaluate which emerging technologies are genuinely appropriate for their context — and implement them within a coherent learning ecosystem strategy.
