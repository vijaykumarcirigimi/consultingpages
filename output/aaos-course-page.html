Frequently Asked Questions About AAOS Training

Everything you need to know before enrolling your team

What is Android Automotive OS and how does it differ from Android Auto?

+

Android Automotive OS (AAOS) is a full operating system built into the vehicle's infotainment hardware, running natively on the head unit. Android Auto, by contrast, is a projection protocol that mirrors a phone's interface onto the car's display. AAOS gives developers direct access to vehicle sensors, VHAL properties, and system-level APIs — enabling deeper integration with vehicle hardware than Android Auto allows.

Who should attend this AAOS training?

+

This training is designed for Android developers, automotive software engineers, embedded systems engineers, platform engineers, and QA engineers who need to build, customize, or test applications and system components for Android Automotive OS. Engineering managers overseeing AAOS adoption projects also benefit from the architectural overview modules.

What prerequisites are required before attending?

+

Participants should have intermediate-level proficiency in Android development (Java or Kotlin), familiarity with the Android SDK and build tools, and a basic understanding of Linux command-line operations. Prior experience with embedded systems or automotive software is helpful but not required — the course covers AAOS-specific concepts from the ground up.

How is the training delivered?

+

We offer three delivery modes: live virtual instructor-led training (VILT) accessible globally, on-site training at your facility for direct team collaboration, and off-site training at a dedicated learning venue. All modes include the same hands-on labs, AAOS emulator exercises, and capstone projects.

What hands-on labs and projects are included?

+

Participants complete 5 capstone projects: building a custom AAOS launcher app, implementing VHAL properties for climate control simulation, creating a media service with car audio focus management, writing a CTS-compatible test suite for a custom HAL, and deploying an OTA update pipeline. All labs run on AAOS emulators and vehicle simulators.

Do participants receive a certification upon completion?

+

Yes. Participants who complete all modules and the final assessment receive an Edstellar Certificate of Completion in Android Automotive OS Development, validating proficiency in AAOS architecture, VHAL implementation, automotive application development, and testing frameworks.

Can the curriculum be customized for our specific OEM platform?

+

Absolutely. We routinely customize the curriculum for specific OEM platforms, vehicle types (EV, ICE, hybrid), and organizational tech stacks. Customization includes adapting VHAL labs to your vehicle's property set, incorporating your build system configurations, and creating case studies relevant to your product line.

What post-training support is provided?

After training, participants receive 90-day access to recorded sessions, membership in a private Slack community with peers and the trainer, monthly 1-hour live Q&A office hours for 3 months, and access to quarterly refresher micro-modules covering new AAOS releases and best practices.
