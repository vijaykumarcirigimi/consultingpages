# Image Sourcing Rules Guide — Edstellar Consulting Pages

> A reference for finding, selecting, and placing images across all consulting page sections. Use this when replacing placeholder image slots in assembled pages.

---

## The 7 Image Categories

Every image on a consulting page falls into one of these categories. Knowing the category tells you exactly what to search for.

---

### 1. HUMAN / PEOPLE images

**When to use:** Sections about leadership, teams, client relationships, consulting delivery, or any content where the message is about people, roles, or organisational impact.

**What to search for:** Adapt these examples based on the specific consulting service the page covers. The search terms below are starting points — modify them to match the exact topic, audience, and tone of your page.

| Content context | Example search terms |
|---|---|
| L&D / training services | e.g. `corporate training workshop professional`, `team learning session office`, or similar terms that show professionals engaged in a learning/training setting |
| Leadership development | e.g. `executive boardroom discussion`, `senior leader presenting strategy`, or similar images showing senior professionals in strategic settings |
| Talent assessment | e.g. `HR manager reviewing employee data`, `professional assessment interview`, or similar scenes of evaluation and review |
| Change management | e.g. `team collaboration transformation`, `diverse team strategy meeting`, or similar visuals of teams navigating change |
| Compliance training | e.g. `compliance officer reviewing documents`, `regulatory training classroom`, or similar regulatory/formal training settings |
| Skills gap analysis | e.g. `HR analytics dashboard professional`, `workforce planning meeting`, or similar data-driven workforce scenes |
| Hero sections (general) | e.g. `business consultant client meeting`, `professional consulting discussion`, or similar — adapt to the specific service |
| Form section | e.g. `friendly consultant with laptop`, `approachable professional office`, or similar warm, welcoming professional images |
| Success stories | e.g. `team celebration achievement`, `handshake business partnership`, or similar images conveying results and partnership |

> **Adapt for your page:** If the consulting page is about a niche topic (e.g., AI upskilling, ESG compliance, supply chain training), adjust the search terms to include that specific context rather than using generic terms.

**Rules:**
- Always use diverse, professional settings (no stock photo clichés like pointing at screens)
- Prefer candid-style over posed — natural meeting moments, active discussions
- Match seniority to audience: CXO-targeted pages need senior-looking professionals, not interns
- Avoid generic "happy office" images — the scene should feel like a real consulting engagement
- For India/APAC-targeted pages, use regionally appropriate professionals

---

### 2. CONCEPTUAL / FRAMEWORK images

**When to use:** Sections explaining methodologies, frameworks, strategies, or abstract business concepts that benefit from a visual representation.

**What to search for:** These are examples for common consulting frameworks. For your specific page, identify the key framework or methodology being discussed and search for that — or something visually similar.

| Content context | Example search terms |
|---|---|
| L&D strategy | e.g. `learning development strategy framework diagram`, `L&D maturity model`, or any visual that represents strategic L&D planning |
| SWOT / gap analysis | e.g. `SWOT analysis diagram professional`, `gap analysis framework visual`, or similar 2x2 or matrix-style visuals |
| Competency mapping | e.g. `competency framework matrix`, `skills competency model diagram`, or similar structured capability visuals |
| ROI measurement | e.g. `training ROI measurement framework`, `learning impact model`, or similar results/measurement visuals |
| Change models | e.g. `ADKAR change model`, `Kotter 8 step change model diagram`, or the specific change model referenced in your content |
| Evaluation models | e.g. `Kirkpatrick four levels evaluation`, `training evaluation model`, or whichever evaluation framework your page discusses |
| Talent pipeline | e.g. `talent pipeline succession framework`, `leadership pipeline model`, or similar pipeline/funnel visuals |
| Risk assessment | e.g. `enterprise risk assessment matrix`, `risk heat map business`, or similar risk-scoring visuals |
| Process / methodology | e.g. `consulting process methodology diagram`, `5 step consulting framework`, or similar step-based visuals |

> **Adapt for your page:** If the page discusses a proprietary or less common framework, search for the closest well-known equivalent and adapt, or create a custom diagram using the Edstellar brand palette.

**Rules:**
- Prefer clean, professional diagrams over busy infographics
- If using a well-known framework (SWOT, Kirkpatrick, etc.), make sure the image accurately represents the model
- Edstellar brand colours can be applied to custom diagrams later — don't worry about colour matching at sourcing stage
- Avoid diagrams with competitor logos or branding
- For service detail sections, the conceptual image should represent what the capability IS, not what it looks like

### 3. TOOL / SOFTWARE / PRODUCT images

**When to use:** Sections referencing specific technologies, platforms, tools, or software that are part of the consulting delivery or the client's environment.

**What to search for:** These are examples for common tool categories. Search for the specific tool or platform mentioned in your content — or a generic version of that tool category if the content is non-specific.

| Content context | Example search terms |
|---|---|
| LMS platforms | e.g. `learning management system dashboard`, `LMS platform interface`, or the specific LMS name if mentioned |
| HR tech / HCM | e.g. `SAP SuccessFactors dashboard`, `Workday HCM interface`, or similar HR platform interfaces relevant to the page |
| Assessment platforms | e.g. `skills assessment platform interface`, `competency testing software`, or any tool that visually represents assessment |
| Analytics / BI tools | e.g. `Power BI HR analytics dashboard`, `Tableau workforce dashboard`, or similar data visualisation tool interfaces |
| AI tools | e.g. `ChatGPT interface`, `AI chatbot enterprise`, or whichever AI tool the content references |
| Project management | e.g. `Jira board project management`, `Asana task board`, or the specific PM tool referenced |
| Edstellar platform | Use actual screenshots from edstellar.com or internal assets |
| Compliance tools | e.g. `compliance management software dashboard`, `regulatory tracking system`, or similar compliance platforms |
| Survey / feedback | e.g. `employee engagement survey platform`, `360 feedback tool interface`, or similar feedback tool UIs |

> **Adapt for your page:** If the page mentions a specific tool by name, search for that tool's official screenshots or press kit images. If it references a generic tool category (e.g., "LMS" or "assessment platform"), use a generic dashboard mockup that looks clean and professional without showing a specific vendor.

**Rules:**
- ALWAYS verify licensing for tool/software screenshots — use official press kits or create mockups
- For Edstellar's own platform, use real screenshots (coordinate with product team)
- Don't use competitor consulting firm logos/screenshots
- If referencing a generic category (e.g., "LMS"), use a generic dashboard mockup rather than a specific vendor
- Tool images work best in service detail sections and accordion blocks

### 4. DATA / METRICS / CHART images

**When to use:** Sections presenting results, statistics, benchmarks, or analytical outcomes — especially success stories, stats sections, and any ROI-focused content.

**What to search for:** These are examples for common data/chart contexts. Adapt the search terms to match the specific metrics, KPIs, or outcomes discussed on your consulting page.

| Content context | Example search terms |
|---|---|
| Training ROI | e.g. `training ROI chart upward trend`, `learning investment return graph`, or similar upward-trending results visuals |
| Skills gap data | e.g. `skills gap analysis chart`, `workforce capability heat map`, or similar capability-mapping visuals |
| Employee engagement | e.g. `employee engagement survey results chart`, `satisfaction score graph`, or similar engagement/sentiment visuals |
| Performance metrics | e.g. `performance improvement metrics dashboard`, `KPI dashboard business`, or similar dashboard views showing progress |
| Benchmark data | e.g. `industry benchmark comparison chart`, `competitive analysis graph`, or similar comparison-style visuals |
| Compliance scores | e.g. `compliance audit pass rate chart`, `regulatory score improvement`, or similar compliance outcome visuals |
| Succession planning | e.g. `leadership readiness pipeline chart`, `succession planning dashboard`, or similar talent pipeline visuals |

> **Adapt for your page:** If the page features specific KPIs (e.g., "time-to-competency", "learning adoption rate"), search for chart visuals that represent those metrics. For success story sections, look for before/after improvement charts that match the story being told.

**Rules:**
- Charts should trend upward or show improvement — never use declining graphs on consulting pages
- Remove or blur any specific company data visible in sourced charts
- Prefer clean, minimal chart styles over dense Excel-style tables
- For success stories, charts showing before/after improvement are most powerful
- Custom charts using Edstellar's brand colours (navy bars, lime highlights) are ideal — create these when possible

### 5. INDUSTRY / SECTOR images

**When to use:** Industries section, or any content targeting a specific vertical (manufacturing, healthcare, BFSI, etc.).

**What to search for:** These are examples for common industries. If your page targets an industry not listed here, apply the same pattern — search for modern professional imagery showing that industry's work environment with a consulting/training angle.

| Industry | Example search terms |
|---|---|
| IT & Technology | e.g. `modern tech office server room`, `software development team`, `cloud computing data center`, or similar tech workplace visuals |
| BFSI | e.g. `banking trading floor`, `financial services office`, `insurance professional meeting`, or similar finance-sector settings |
| Manufacturing | e.g. `modern factory floor automation`, `manufacturing quality control`, `industrial engineer`, or similar modern production settings |
| Healthcare | e.g. `hospital medical team`, `healthcare professional patient`, `clinical training simulation`, or similar medical/clinical visuals |
| Retail | e.g. `retail store operations`, `e-commerce warehouse`, `customer experience retail`, or similar retail/commerce settings |
| Energy | e.g. `renewable energy solar wind`, `oil gas operations`, `power plant control room`, or similar energy-sector visuals |
| Telecom | e.g. `5G tower infrastructure`, `telecom network operations center`, `fiber optic engineer`, or similar telecom infrastructure visuals |
| Pharma | e.g. `pharmaceutical lab research`, `drug manufacturing clean room`, `clinical trial research`, or similar lab/research visuals |
| Government | e.g. `government office administration`, `public sector digital services`, `civic building modern`, or similar public-sector settings |

> **Adapt for your page:** For niche industries (e.g., logistics, aviation, edtech, agritech), search for that industry's professional environment + a consulting or training context. The goal is always to show professionals in that sector, not just the sector's products.

**Rules:**
- Industry images must look modern and progressive — avoid dated industrial imagery
- For the industries section, icons are already built into the designs (SVG), so images are only needed if using the selectable-tiles variant's detail panel
- Match the image to the CONSULTING context, not just the industry — show professionals in that industry, not just the industry itself
- For global pages, use internationally recognisable settings

### 6. WORKSPACE / ENVIRONMENT images

**When to use:** Background or ambient images for hero sections, form sections, or download asset sections where the image sets the mood rather than illustrating a specific concept.

**What to search for:** These are examples for common workspace contexts. Adapt based on the consulting page's audience and tone — a CXO-targeted page needs a more premium setting than a general L&D page.

| Content context | Example search terms |
|---|---|
| Hero background | e.g. `modern corporate office bright`, `consulting firm office interior`, or similar professional spaces that match your page's tone |
| Form section | e.g. `professional meeting room welcoming`, `modern office reception area`, or similar inviting, approachable settings |
| Download asset | e.g. `professional desk with documents laptop`, `report template workspace`, or similar clean desk/document visuals |
| General consulting | e.g. `business strategy whiteboard session`, `post-it notes brainstorming wall`, or similar collaborative work-in-progress scenes |
| Executive retreat | e.g. `premium conference venue`, `executive retreat location meeting`, or similar high-end professional venues |
| Training facility | e.g. `corporate training room modern`, `learning center classroom`, or similar modern training environments |

> **Adapt for your page:** The workspace should reflect the page's target audience. Pages targeting senior executives need boardrooms and premium venues. Pages targeting HR managers need modern, collaborative office spaces. Pages targeting frontline teams need workshop and training room settings.

**Rules:**
- Workspace images should feel aspirational but realistic
- Avoid overly sterile or empty spaces — some human presence (even blurred) adds warmth
- Prefer natural light over harsh fluorescent settings
- The environment should match the seniority of the target audience — CXO pages need premium settings

---

### 7. ICON / ILLUSTRATION images

**When to use:** When you need abstract visual elements that don't require photography — decorative icons, illustrations, or stylised graphics.

**What to search for:** These are examples for common illustration needs. Adapt based on what abstract concept or decorative element the page requires.

| Content context | Example search terms |
|---|---|
| Service icons | e.g. `business consulting icon set flat`, `corporate training icon set`, or similar icon sets matching your service category |
| Process steps | e.g. `workflow step illustration flat`, `process step icons business`, or similar step-based illustrative elements |
| Abstract concepts | e.g. `digital transformation illustration`, `innovation abstract business`, or visuals representing the page's core concept |
| Isometric illustrations | e.g. `business consulting isometric illustration`, `team collaboration isometric`, or similar 3D-style business visuals |
| Line art | e.g. `business strategy line art minimal`, `corporate training line illustration`, or similar minimal line-based visuals |

> **Adapt for your page:** If the page covers a specific concept (e.g., "organisational agility", "data governance"), search for illustrations of that concept. Keep a consistent illustration style across the whole page — don't mix flat, isometric, and line art.

**Rules:**
- The library designs already include SVG icons — only source illustrations for custom needs
- If using illustrations, maintain a consistent style across the entire page (don't mix flat + isometric + 3D)
- Match the illustration style to the brand personality — Edstellar is professional, modern, and warm (not playful or cartoon-like)
- For custom illustrations, the colour palette should use navy, lime, and neutral tones

## Sourcing Priority

1. **Unsplash** — unsplash.com — royalty-free, no attribution required, high quality professional photography
2. **Pexels** — pexels.com — royalty-free, no attribution required, strong business/corporate category
3. **Pixabay** — pixabay.com — royalty-free, no attribution required, good for conceptual and abstract visuals
4. **Rawpixel (free section)** — rawpixel.com/free — royalty-free, design resources and photos
5. **StockSnap** — stocksnap.io — royalty-free, CC0 license, curated business photography
6. **Burst by Shopify** — burst.shopify.com — royalty-free, strong in business and workplace categories
