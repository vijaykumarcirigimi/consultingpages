## SECTION 02 │ What Are Training Simulations, AR/VR & Case Studies?

**SECTION LABEL:** Understanding the Discipline

### What Are Training Simulations, AR/VR & Case Studies?
 🔍 SEO: H2 KW: 'Training Simulations, AR/VR & Case Studies' — definitional section · KW: 'training simulations', 'AR VR training', 'case study development', 'simulation-based training', 'experiential learning design', 'scenario-based learning'

Training simulations, AR/VR, and case studies are experiential learning formats that replace passive instruction with active practice. **Training simulations** use technology to recreate real-world scenarios — from operating machinery and handling customer complaints to running a business unit — in a safe, consequence-free environment where learners build competence through doing. **AR and VR training** takes simulation to the next level: virtual reality immerses learners in fully digital environments for high-stakes practice, while augmented reality overlays digital guidance onto the real world for on-the-job performance support. **Case studies** are structured decision-making exercises built around real or realistic business scenarios, challenging learners to analyze, debate, and decide — building the strategic judgment that no classroom lecture can teach. Together, these formats represent the full spectrum of experiential learning design: from the physical realism of VR to the strategic depth of a Harvard-method case study, each format serves a different learning need. The consulting discipline is choosing which format fits which skill, audience, and business outcome — and designing it to the quality standard that changes behavior.

## SECTION 21 │ FAQ

### Frequently Asked Questions About Training Simulations, AR/VR & Case Studies
 🔍 SEO: H2 KW: 'Training Simulations, AR/VR & Case Studies' — FAQ targets long-tail KWs: 'VR vs AR vs desktop simulations', 'cost VR training', 'VR AR hardware enterprise', 'ROI simulation training', 'simulation LMS integration'

**Q1: When should we use VR vs. AR vs. desktop simulations vs. case studies?**
The modality should match the skill and risk profile. Use **VR** when physical fidelity matters and the real environment is dangerous, expensive, or impossible to replicate (safety procedures, surgical training, emergency response). Use **AR** when workers need real-time guidance in the physical world (field maintenance, assembly, warehouse operations). Use **desktop/browser simulations** when you need scalable practice for customer service, compliance, software skills, or process training — without VR hardware costs. Use **business simulations** for leadership, finance, and strategic decision-making skills that require seeing P&L consequences. Use **case studies** for deep strategic analysis, executive education, and situations where debate and deliberation are the learning mechanism. Many programs benefit from a blended approach — VR for the physical procedure, desktop scenarios for the decision-making, and a case study for the strategic context.

**Q2: How much does it cost to develop a custom VR training experience?**
Costs vary significantly by complexity. A 360° video experience (real environment filmed and annotated) typically costs $15,000-$50,000. A basic interactive VR simulation (single environment, limited branching) ranges from $50,000-$150,000. A complex, multi-scenario VR training program with custom 3D environments, advanced branching, analytics, and multi-hardware deployment ranges from $150,000-$500,000+. For comparison, a high-quality desktop branching simulation costs $20,000-$80,000, a business simulation $30,000-$120,000, and a custom case study $8,000-$25,000. The key is matching complexity to learning need — Edstellar's modality assessment ensures you don't overspend where a simpler format would work.

**Q3: What VR/AR hardware do we need for enterprise training deployment?**
The most common enterprise VR hardware today is Meta Quest (standalone, no PC required) — cost-effective and easy to deploy at scale. Apple Vision Pro offers premium mixed-reality for executive/specialized use cases. HTC Vive and Pico serve enterprise deployments with specific requirements. For AR, standard smartphones and tablets work for most applications; dedicated AR glasses (Microsoft HoloLens, Magic Leap) are used for hands-free industrial applications. Edstellar assesses your infrastructure, scale requirements, and IT policies before recommending hardware — and designs all VR content with a 2D browser-based fallback so non-VR users can still access the training.

**Q4: How do you measure the ROI of simulation-based training?**
We apply the same rigorous measurement approach used across Edstellar's consulting practice. At **Level 1**, we measure learner engagement and satisfaction with the simulation experience. At **Level 2**, pre/post assessments and in-simulation performance scores measure knowledge and skill acquisition. At **Level 3**, manager assessments and on-the-job observations 60-90 days post-training verify behavior change. At **Level 4**, we track business impact metrics — safety incident reduction, error rates, time-to-competency, customer satisfaction, and sales performance. At **Level 5** (ROI), we calculate the financial return by comparing program benefits (quantified business impact) against total costs (development, hardware, deployment, maintenance). For high-stakes programs, we conduct full ROI impact studies with isolation techniques to separate the simulation's contribution from other variables.

**Q5: Can simulations integrate with our existing LMS?**
Yes. All desktop-based simulations, branching scenarios, and game-based learning are built to SCORM 1.2/2004 and xAPI (Tin Can) standards for seamless LMS integration. Completion status, assessment scores, time spent, and branching path data flow directly into your LMS reporting. For VR/AR experiences, we integrate analytics via xAPI to the LMS or a Learning Record Store (LRS), capturing session data, completion status, performance scores, and behavioral metrics. Business simulations and case studies can be tracked through LMS-integrated assessment rubrics and facilitator-submitted evaluation forms.

**Q6: How long does it take to develop a custom simulation from scratch?**
Timeline depends on modality and complexity. A custom **case study** takes 4-8 weeks (research, writing, facilitator guide). A **branching-scenario eLearning** module takes 6-10 weeks. A **business simulation** game takes 8-14 weeks. A **desktop software simulation** takes 6-12 weeks. A **360° video VR** experience takes 6-10 weeks. A **fully interactive VR** training program takes 12-20 weeks depending on environment complexity and number of scenarios. These timelines include our full five-phase methodology (Assess → Design → Develop → Pilot → Deploy). Edstellar's AI-enhanced design tools can accelerate scenario writing and branching logic development by 30-40%, shortening timelines for large-scale projects.
