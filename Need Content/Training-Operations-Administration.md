## SECTION 02 │ What Is Training Operations & Administration?

**SECTION LABEL:** Understanding the Discipline

### What Is Training Operations & Administration?

Training operations and administration is the comprehensive management of every logistical, technological, and administrative process required to deliver enterprise learning programs at scale. It encompasses the full operational lifecycle of training — from scheduling sessions and managing instructors to administering learning management systems, processing enrollments, coordinating materials and facilities, providing learner support, and tracking compliance. **Training operations** refers to the coordination of people, processes, and technology that make training delivery possible — session scheduling, instructor assignment, facility booking, materials distribution, and quality assurance. **Training administration** covers the system-level management functions — LMS configuration, user management, data entry, completion tracking, reporting, and regulatory documentation. Together, these functions form the operational engine that sits beneath every learning program an organization delivers. When training operations run efficiently, learners receive the right training at the right time in the right format. When they break down, programs stall, compliance gaps emerge, costs escalate, and the L&D team spends its time firefighting logistics instead of designing impactful learning experiences.

## SECTION 20 │ FAQ

### Frequently Asked Questions About Training Operations & Administration

**Q1: What is the difference between managed training services and training operations outsourcing?**
Managed training services (MTS) is a broad category that encompasses the outsourcing of any part of the learning and development function — including content development, learning strategy, technology, and delivery. Training operations outsourcing specifically focuses on the administrative and logistical functions that support training delivery — LMS administration, scheduling, enrollment management, instructor coordination, learner support, and compliance tracking. Think of training operations as the operational engine that sits beneath the strategic layer. An organization might outsource training operations while keeping strategy, content development, and program design in-house. Edstellar offers both comprehensive managed training services and targeted training operations outsourcing, depending on your organization's needs and operating model preferences. Many clients begin with operations outsourcing and expand to additional managed services as they experience the efficiency gains.

**Q2: How do you ensure data security and privacy when managing our LMS and learner data?**
Data security is foundational to our training operations service. Edstellar operates under enterprise-grade security protocols including SOC 2 Type II compliance, GDPR data processing agreements, and industry-specific regulations such as HIPAA for healthcare clients. Our administrators access client systems through secure, audited connections with role-based permissions that limit access to only the data and functions required for their role. All access is logged and auditable. We sign comprehensive data processing agreements with every client, and our security practices are reviewed annually by third-party auditors. For clients with heightened security requirements, we offer dedicated infrastructure, VPN-only access, and background-checked personnel with security clearance where required.

**Q3: Can you manage our LMS if we use a platform your team has not worked with before?**
Yes. Our LMS administrators are trained across all major learning technology platforms and have deep expertise in Cornerstone, SAP SuccessFactors, Workday Learning, Docebo, Absorb, Moodle, Totara, Saba, SumTotal, and many others. For platforms we have not previously administered, we conduct a structured ramp-up process — including vendor training, documentation review, sandbox environment practice, and a supervised parallel operation period — before assuming full administrative responsibility. Our platform-agnostic approach means you are never locked into a technology decision based on your operations provider's limitations. We have historically ramped up to full operational proficiency on new platforms within 3-4 weeks.

**Q4: How quickly can you transition our training operations to a managed model?**
Our standard transition timeline is 6-10 weeks from contract signature to steady-state operations — though this varies based on scope and complexity. The transition follows a structured process: weeks 1-2 for assessment and knowledge transfer, weeks 3-4 for process design and SOP documentation, weeks 5-6 for staff onboarding and technology access, and weeks 7-10 for parallel operations and quality validation. For urgent transitions — such as replacing an incumbent provider or responding to a sudden capacity gap — we offer an accelerated 4-week transition track that leverages pre-built playbooks and experienced transition managers. Throughout the transition, your existing training delivery continues uninterrupted.

**Q5: What does your variable cost model look like, and how does pricing work?**
Edstellar's training operations pricing is based on a variable cost model tied to actual operational volume — not fixed headcount. We price based on measurable transaction units: enrollments processed, sessions scheduled, LMS transactions managed, helpdesk tickets resolved, and reports produced. This means your costs flex with your actual training demand — scaling up during peak periods like annual compliance cycles or new hire surges, and scaling down during quieter periods. The model typically includes a baseline retainer covering minimum operational capacity plus variable fees for volume above baseline. Most clients see 25-35% cost reduction compared to their previous fully loaded internal or outsourced model, with the additional benefit of converting fixed headcount costs into variable operating expenses.

**Q6: How do you handle training operations for organizations with multiple LMS platforms across regions?**
Multi-platform environments are common among our enterprise clients — we currently manage operations across organizations with 2-7 concurrent learning platforms. Our approach treats the multi-platform landscape as a single unified operation from the learner's and stakeholder's perspective, even though administration occurs across multiple systems. We assign platform-specialized administrators to each system while maintaining a unified operations management layer that coordinates scheduling, enrollment, reporting, and compliance tracking across all platforms. Data reconciliation processes ensure consistent learner records across systems, and our unified reporting dashboards aggregate data from all platforms into a single operational view. For clients planning platform consolidation, our multi-platform operational experience also provides valuable insight into migration planning and data cleansing.
