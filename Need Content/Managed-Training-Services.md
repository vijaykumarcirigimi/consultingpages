## SECTION 02 | What Are Managed Training Services?

**SECTION LABEL:** Understanding the Discipline

### What Are Managed Training Services?

Managed training services is a strategic outsourcing model where an organization partners with a specialized managed training services provider to plan, execute, and manage some or all of its learning and development operations. Unlike traditional training procurement, where companies purchase individual courses or hire ad hoc contractors, managed training services consulting delivers a comprehensive, end-to-end operating model that covers the full L&D value chain: from training needs analysis and content development through scheduling, delivery, vendor management, technology administration, and performance measurement.

At its core, managed training services consulting transforms the L&D function from a fragmented collection of activities into a streamlined, professionally managed operation. The managed training services company acts as an embedded extension of the client's team, bringing specialized expertise, established processes, global delivery infrastructure, and economies of scale that most internal L&D departments cannot replicate alone. This model enables enterprises to maintain strategic control over learning priorities while outsourcing the operational complexity that drains internal resources, inflates costs, and limits scalability.

The scope of a managed training services engagement can range from a single function, such as training administration or vendor management, to a full learning business process outsourcing (learning BPO) arrangement where the provider assumes responsibility for the entire learning operation. Leading managed training solutions providers like Edstellar tailor the engagement model to each client's maturity level, budget constraints, and strategic objectives, ensuring the outsourced operation delivers continuous improvement in cost efficiency, learner experience, and business impact.

## SECTION 20 | FAQ

### Frequently Asked Questions About Managed Training Services

**Q1: What is the difference between managed training services and traditional training outsourcing?**
Traditional training outsourcing typically involves purchasing individual training programs, hiring contract trainers, or outsourcing a single function like LMS administration on a project-by-project basis. Managed training services is a fundamentally different model: it is a strategic, ongoing partnership where the managed training services provider assumes operational responsibility for multiple L&D functions under a unified governance framework with defined SLAs, performance metrics, and continuous improvement commitments. The provider operates as an extension of your L&D team, managing processes end-to-end rather than delivering discrete projects. The key differences are scope (comprehensive vs. piecemeal), duration (ongoing partnership vs. project engagement), accountability (SLA-governed vs. deliverable-based), and value creation (continuous optimization vs. one-time execution). Organizations that shift from traditional outsourcing to managed training services typically see 25-40% improvement in cost efficiency, faster delivery cycles, higher quality consistency, and significantly better data visibility across their learning operations.

**Q2: How much can our organization save by adopting managed training services?**
Cost savings from managed training services consulting engagements vary based on organizational size, current operational maturity, and engagement scope, but industry benchmarks and Edstellar client data consistently show 25-40% reduction in total training operational costs. These savings come from multiple sources: vendor portfolio rationalization typically saves 20-35% on supplier spend by consolidating contracts and negotiating volume pricing; operational process automation reduces administrative labor costs by 30-50%; delivery format optimization (shifting from high-cost classroom events to blended and virtual models where appropriate) saves 15-25% on delivery costs; and technology consolidation eliminates redundant platform licenses and reduces IT support overhead. Beyond direct cost savings, managed training services also deliver indirect financial benefits: faster onboarding reduces time-to-productivity, improved compliance training reduces regulatory risk and penalty exposure, and better analytics enable data-driven investment decisions that eliminate spending on low-impact programs. We provide a detailed savings projection during the assessment phase before any engagement commitment.

**Q3: How long does the transition to managed training services take?**
A typical transition to managed training services takes 8-16 weeks from engagement kickoff to full operational management, depending on the scope of services being transitioned and the complexity of the existing operation. The transition follows a phased approach: the Assessment phase (2-4 weeks) audits your current operations and identifies the target operating model; the Design phase (3-4 weeks) defines SLAs, governance, and transition plans; and the Transition phase (4-8 weeks) executes the phased handover of operational workstreams. During transition, we operate in parallel with your existing team to ensure zero disruption to ongoing training delivery. Critical workstreams like compliance training and active program delivery are never interrupted. Most clients see initial operational improvements within the first 60 days, with full steady-state optimization achieved by month 6.

**Q4: Will we lose control of our L&D strategy if we outsource training operations?**
No. Managed training services is explicitly designed to separate strategic control from operational execution. Your organization retains full authority over learning strategy, program priorities, budget allocation, and business alignment decisions. Edstellar manages the operational execution: the scheduling, logistics, vendor management, technology administration, delivery coordination, and reporting that consume most of your team's time today. Our governance framework defines clear decision-making authorities, escalation paths, and review cadences that keep you in control of every strategic decision while we handle the operational complexity. In practice, most clients find they have more strategic control after adopting managed training services because their internal team is finally freed from administrative overload and has the time, data, and bandwidth to focus on strategic priorities they previously neglected.

**Q5: What types of organizations benefit most from managed training services?**
Managed training services deliver the greatest impact for organizations with 2,000+ employees, multi-regional operations, complex regulatory training requirements, or L&D teams that have reached operational capacity limits. Common triggers include: rapid growth outpacing L&D headcount, M&A activity requiring integration of multiple training operations, compliance training failures or audit findings, executive demands for training cost transparency, technology platform migrations, and L&D teams spending more time on administration than strategy. Industry sectors with the highest adoption rates include financial services, technology, healthcare, manufacturing, and energy, but managed training services solutions are effective in any organization where L&D operations have become too complex or resource-intensive for the internal team to manage efficiently.

**Q6: How do you ensure training quality remains consistent across all regions and delivery modes?**
Quality consistency is built into every layer of our managed training services operating model. For instructor-led delivery, every facilitator passes a multi-step qualification process including domain expertise validation, facilitation skills assessment, observed delivery sessions, and ongoing learner feedback scoring. Standardized facilitation guides, delivery checklists, and quality rubrics ensure every session follows the same instructional framework regardless of location. For content, all learning materials go through a structured design review, SME validation, and QA process before deployment. We conduct mystery learner evaluations, calibration sessions across instructors, and quarterly quality reviews by domain. Our analytics platform tracks quality metrics in real time: learner satisfaction scores, knowledge assessment pass rates, and behavioral application ratings, flagging any instructor, program, or region that falls below threshold. If a quality issue is detected, our corrective action process triggers within 24 hours.
